Executive

Executives negotiate how much money their organization will get with other top level leaders or clients. They may also be involved in contracts, create projects, choose vendors and meet with other executives throughout the industry. They are also master sales people, visionaries and excellent negotiators.
  • VP of Software Development

Management

Managers are responsible for hiring, firing, performance reviews and are generally allocated a department budget. They spend most of their time managing people as opposed to making technology decisions.
  • Development Sr. Director
  • Development Director
  • Development Sr. Manager
  • Development Manager
  • Operations Manager
  • Testing Manager
  • Support Manager
  • Support Supervisor

Software Design

Software Design is responsible for making decisions on which technologies will be used throughout the company and on behalf of their clients. They are also involved in industry working committees, may work on contract technology definition, do research for executives and participate in industry events. Decisions that they make span more than one department and more than one project and possibly more than one company.
  • Fellow Architect
  • Distinguished Architect
  • Senior Principal Architect
  • Principal Architect
  • Software Architect

Software Development

Software Development is responsible for ideation, analysis, design, development, test, production delivery and warranty support. They may also fix long standing bugs based on bug reports. There may be a balance between new product development, upgrading legacy code and technologies, fixing bugs and lifecycle development. At higher levels, a lead may focus more on analysis, design, development of shared libraries, code reviews and overseeing other developers.
  • Senior Lead Software Engineer
  • Lead Software Engineer
  • Senior Software Engineer
  • Software Engineer
  • Associate Software Engineer

User Interface Development

User interface designers and developers focus on any part of the software that the customer can actually see. For example, page layout, color schemes and graphical images need to be chosen and written. Projects that are not customer facing will not need this team. They often produce wireframes and communicate through an API gateway. For security reasons, it is critical that the UI have the least amount of business logic possible.
  • UX Designer
  • Graphic Designer
  • Lead UI Software Engineer
  • Senior UI Software Engineer
  • UI Software Engineer
  • Associate UI Software Engineer
